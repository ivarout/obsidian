
 - **[Japan Open-Air Folk House Museum](https://www.google.com/search?client=firefox-b-d&q=Japan+Open-Air+Folk+House+Museum&mstk=AUtExfBHke0e3C1VFViJ4Eg0CYFR5mMQqwa3YKmHRXNK_nBKzzYXDrneLrWpI_ttMH2nCYWUaRXbve6MRIanIkjCr-cmwDlwfFrmEQ2wkZfgLwBYZJd7OgEhQO22-syi2yKXE82u8Xyqu02snf6hjw8vloTBhPZTALwxL4Z5QSI1K8TbVNU&csui=3&ved=2ahUKEwiP1KzKoP6SAxUwslYBHRd6HTQQgK4QegQIAxAG) (Kawasaki):** A hidden gem near Noborito station with traditional thatched-roof houses relocated from all over Japan in a quiet park setting.
- **Mt. Nokogiri (Chiba):** Take the ferry from Kurihama to Kanaya for a unique approach. Hike up to see the massive 31-meter Buddha"Jigoku Nozoki" (view of hell) cliff edge, and 1,500 Arhat statues.
- **Ome & Okutama (Western Tokyo):** Known as "Tokyo's backyard" take the Ome line to Hike Mt. Mitake, visit the Okutama Lake area, or explore the Retro-themed town of Ome.
- **Tatebayashi & Ashikaga (Gunma/Tochigi):** Skip the main Ashikaga Flower Park during peak season and visit the surrounding areas. Tatebayashi is known for its azalea park, old samurai houses, and soy sauce breweries.
- **[Katsunuma](https://www.google.com/search?client=firefox-b-d&q=Katsunuma&mstk=AUtExfBHke0e3C1VFViJ4Eg0CYFR5mMQqwa3YKmHRXNK_nBKzzYXDrneLrWpI_ttMH2nCYWUaRXbve6MRIanIkjCr-cmwDlwfFrmEQ2wkZfgLwBYZJd7OgEhQO22-syi2yKXE82u8Xyqu02snf6hjw8vloTBhPZTALwxL4Z5QSI1K8TbVNU&csui=3&ved=2ahUKEwiP1KzKoP6SAxUwslYBHRd6HTQQgK4QegQIAxAE) Wineries (Yamanashi):** A relaxing day trip to Japan's wine country. Visit various small wineries in the Yamanashi region, easily accessible by the Chuo Line.
- **[Tsukuba-san](https://www.google.com/search?client=firefox-b-d&q=Tsukuba-san&mstk=AUtExfBHke0e3C1VFViJ4Eg0CYFR5mMQqwa3YKmHRXNK_nBKzzYXDrneLrWpI_ttMH2nCYWUaRXbve6MRIanIkjCr-cmwDlwfFrmEQ2wkZfgLwBYZJd7OgEhQO22-syi2yKXE82u8Xyqu02snf6hjw8vloTBhPZTALwxL4Z5QSI1K8TbVNU&csui=3&ved=2ahUKEwiP1KzKoP6SAxUwslYBHRd6HTQQgK4QegQIAxAI) (Ibaraki):** Known as the "purple mountain" hike or take the cable car up for panoramic views of the Kanto Plain and a less-crowded alternative to Mt. Takao.
